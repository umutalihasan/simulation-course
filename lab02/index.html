▸ Temp-Dependent Properties
λ(T) = λ₀ + dλ·T  |  c(T) = c₀ + dc·T
Real materials change conductivity with temperature.
Steel example: λ decreases ~0.015 W/m·°C per °C rise.
At 200°C: λ ≈ 46 − 0.015×200 = 43 W/m·°C
▸ Convection Boundaries
q = h_conv · (T_surface − T_ambient)
Replaces fixed-temp BC with Newton's cooling law.
Typical h values: natural air 5–25, forced air 25–250, water 200–10000 W/m²·°C
▸ Internal Heat Source
Q(x) = Q₀ · exp(−((x−x₀)/σ)²)
Gaussian heat source at position x₀ (e.g. resistance wire, laser).
Positive Q = heat generation (e.g. electric current).
Negative Q = heat sink (e.g. cooling channel).
